UPenn Design Challenge

This past October, the University of Pennsylvania's Integrated Product Design program facilated a 24-gour Design Challenge for students from Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Northwestern University, Carnegie Mellon University, and their own university.

Students from Integrated Design programs from each university were selected to participate in the challenge in Philadephia - which was sponsored in conjunction with Penn Medicine- and given resources to subject matter experts, stakeholders, and facilities to explore the problem statement and develop solutions.

Problem Statement

Eldery patients commonly suffer from Postoperative Cognitive Decline (POCD) or Delirium, a threatening cognitive issue, after waking up from post-surgery anesthesia. This disorder can excite hysteria and lead to long-term cognitive decline for these eldery patients if not properly managed and mitigated. Develop a solution to improve the current state of POCD management and aid its respective stakeholders.

Understanding POCD

The challenge kicked off with a Penn Medicine subject-matter expert presenting the POCD problem. After her initial presentation, participants were given access to materials to explore and understand POCD.

Content was divided between team members, in order to enable the most information consumption in the time allotted.
